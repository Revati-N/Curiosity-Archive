It is the psychobiological phenomenon where a person’s physical or mental health improves by receiving an inactive or “dummy” treatment.

It is driven by the patient’s anticipation, belief in treatment and the overall clinical context rather than the ingredients of the treatment.

“How placebos work is not quite understood, but it involves a complex neurobiological reaction that includes everything from increases in feel-good neurotransmitters to greater activity in certain brain regions linked to moods, emotional reactions and self-awareness.”

Placebos can work in both positive and negative ways. You can do something and convince yourself you will definitely get the results you deserve/want. However, you can also hear/think you have a fever and get it. Sometimes people “placebo” symptoms after just thinking they had contact with a disease when they haven’t in reality.

*Placebo - Positive. Nocebo - Negative.*

Most results are experimental and haven’t been completely confirmed yet.

In reality, placebos impact our perception and not something drastic physically.

Studies have shown that placebos can have an effect on conditions like Depression, Sleep Disorders, pain, irritable bowel syndrome and menopause.
