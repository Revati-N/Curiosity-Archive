Lyric analysis is literary analysis for songs. Once you learn the moves, you start seeing them everywhere.

---

## 1. Start with a Close Read

Listen (or read) slowly — not vibe-listening, _attention_ listening. Notice:

- What's the literal situation? (who's speaking, to whom, about what)
- What's the emotional register? (angry, nostalgic, desperate, ironic?)
- Where does your gut react — a line that hits different, a weird word choice, a surprising turn

---

## 2. Look at Word Choice (Diction)

Lyrics are compressed — every word is doing heavy lifting. Ask:

- Why _this_ word and not a simpler one?
- Are there words that feel out of place? (usually intentional)
- Are there patterns — words from the same semantic field (all water imagery, all money references)?

---

## 3. Find the Devices

|Device|What to look for|
|---|---|
|**Metaphor / Simile**|What's being compared to what — and why does that comparison _mean_ something?|
|**Repetition**|What gets said more than once? Repetition = emphasis, obsession, or a loop you can't escape|
|**Contrast / Juxtaposition**|Two opposing things placed together (e.g. joy + grief in the same line)|
|**Ambiguity**|Lines that can mean two things at once — usually the richest ones to sit with|

---

## 4. Song Structure 101

Most songs are built from the same handful of parts. Here's what they are and what they _do_:

|Part|What it is|What it does|
|---|---|---|
|**Verse**|The storytelling section. Lyrics change each time.|Sets up context, moves the narrative forward, adds detail|
|**Chorus**|The emotional peak. Lyrics are usually the same each time.|Delivers the main message / feeling of the song — the thing you remember|
|**Pre-Chorus**|A short build-up section before the chorus (not all songs have it)|Creates tension and makes the chorus hit harder|
|**Bridge**|A section that breaks the verse-chorus pattern, usually appears once|Shifts perspective, raises stakes, or offers a twist before the final chorus|
|**Outro**|The ending section|Winds the song down, sometimes with a final statement|
|**Hook**|The most catchy, memorable line or phrase (often _in_ the chorus)|The thing stuck in your head — usually the title of the song|
|**Intro**|The opening, often instrumental|Sets the mood before the lyrics begin|

### A typical song structure looks like:

```
Intro → Verse 1 → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Verse 2 → Pre-Chorus → Chorus → Bridge → Chorus → Outro
```

> **Why does this matter for analysis?** _Where_ something is said is as important as _what_ is said. A confession in a bridge lands differently than the same line in a chorus. The bridge is often where the emotional truth of the song lives.

---

## 5. Zoom Out: Structure (Analysis)

- How is the song built? Does it follow a standard structure or break it?
- Does the chorus mean the same thing every time it repeats, or does context shift its meaning?
- Does anything change between the first verse and the last?
- Is there a bridge? What does it reveal that the verses didn't?

---

## 6. Ask the "So What"

The most important move. After noticing something, push further:

- _Why does this choice matter?_
- _What does it say about the speaker's state of mind / worldview / relationship to the subject?_
- _What does it make me feel, and how does the craft create that feeling?_

---

## The Practice Loop

> **Notice → Interrogate → Connect**

Pick one song you already love. Write down 3 lines that hit you. For each one, ask:

> _What's happening here technically, and why does it work emotionally?_

That loop is the whole skill.
