It is a learning task that requires a considerable but desirable amount of effort.

It slows down small-term performance but helps in long-term learning.
> To be desirable, the tasks must be achievable.

Many tasks give the illusion of learning because they are easily available.

When you face a problem, you feel more inclined to solve it and hence the solution gets placed firmly in your brain.

Increasing difficulty helps in learning.
It aids knowledge retention.

**Desirable Difficulties**
* Spacing practice over time
* Interleaving topics/skills
* Retrieval Practice (Testing)
* Generation Before Learning
* Varied practice conditions
* Reducing feedback gradually
