## Main Idea:

Grit = passion + perseverance over the long term.

Duckworth's whole argument is that talent is overrated. The people who succeed aren't necessarily the smartest or most naturally gifted — they're the ones who stick with their goals for years, not days. She calls this "grit" and argues it predicts success better than IQ or talent does.

She studied high achievers across wildly different fields — West Point cadets, spelling bee kids, salespeople — and found the common thread was never raw talent. It was sustained effort over time.

She has a simple formula: talent × effort = skill, skill × effort = achievement. Effort counts twice. Talent without effort is just untapped potential.

Grit is also about having a single "top-level goal" — a life philosophy almost — that organizes everything else under it. Not just grinding randomly, but grinding toward one consistent direction.

The honest bit she ends on: she admits we don't fully know how to build grit in people yet. But the most promising idea is **growth mindset** (Dweck's work) — believing your abilities can develop with effort.

Basically: stop worshipping talent, respect consistency.
