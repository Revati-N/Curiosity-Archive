## Main Idea:

Tim Urban takes the example of his own Master's thesis to show the difference between a normal non-procrastinator and a procrastinator.

He states that the difference between both their brains is that the brain of a procrastinator has a "Instant Gratification Monkey". Basically, the urge to be instantly gratified. 

The "Instant Gratification Monkey" as he calls it only lives in the present. He does not care about the past or the future. He only cares about two things: EASY and FUN. 

There's two kinds of procrastination:
* When tasks have a deadline. (Short Term)
* When tasks don't have a deadline + Without momentum. (Long Term)

He suggests that the best way to actually get the work done is to look at the calendar of an average life and the amount of days/weeks already ticked off.. This would help set a deadline and help the "Panic Machine" to set in.
