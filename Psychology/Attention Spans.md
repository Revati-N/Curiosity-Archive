It is defined as the amount of time a person can stay/remain concentrated on a thing or task at a time.

It generally increases as a child grows up. However, kids and adults are all different and unique so their attention spans can lie separately and are on a spectrum.

With the growth of digital media, it is studied/has been studied that generations who grew up with screens have a much lower attention span as compared to ones without. 

I, however, feel that the attention spans might have reduced due to the increased availability of external stimuli.

With this increase in information, a human has to stop and decide each time whether they wish to scroll further or stop. This choice is often harder than expected because the content available online is made specifically so people keep consuming it. 

Hence, Attention has quite literally become the new currency in the digital era.

This has increased the number of people promoting "Digital Minimalism" and similar coinages.
